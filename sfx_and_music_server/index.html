Prompt Guide

How to write great prompts for each generation type — click any example to use it

General Tips
Be specific & descriptive

The more detail you provide — genre, instruments, mood, tempo — the better the output. Don't just say "jazz"; say "smooth jazz piano trio with brushed drums and walking bass, 95 BPM"

Include BPM for music

Adding tempo like "120 BPM" or "85 BPM" significantly improves rhythmic coherence. Leave it out for ambient/non-rhythmic pieces.

Set a realistic duration

Short durations (15–30s) generate faster and often sound more coherent. Only use long durations when you truly need them — on CPU, 120s takes a long time.

Use AudioSparx tags

Prefixing with TrackType: Music, VocalType: Instrumental or TrackType: SFX steers the model strongly.

Embrace randomness

Use seed=-1 to explore variations. Once you find a seed you like, lock it in for consistent results.

Steps 4 vs 8

On CPU, use steps=4 for quick previews and to test your prompt, then regenerate with steps=8 for the final export. Going above 8 doesn't help with small- models.

🎵 Music Prompting small-music
✓ DO
Describe genre, instruments, mood, tempo, production style, and energy. The more layers of detail, the richer the output. Reference emotional states ("euphoric," "nostalgic," "tense") and sonic textures ("warm vinyl crackle," "fuzzy synth pads").
✗ DON'T
Write vague one-word prompts ("jazz", "epic"), include lyrics or vocal descriptions (the model can only produce unintelligible vocal textures), or ask for copyrighted songs/artists.

EXAMPLE PROMPTS — click to use:

TrackType: Music, VocalType: Instrumental, Genre: Lo-fi Hip Hop. Lazy afternoon lo-fi beat with dusty vinyl crackle, muted Rhodes piano chords, soft brushed snare, and a warm sub-bass groove. Melancholic but cosy mood, 85 BPM
An epic cinematic orchestral build with strings, brass swells, and a thunderous percussion section. Starts with quiet, tense piano and builds into a full triumphant climax. Dramatic and emotionally overwhelming.
TrackType: Music, Genre: Tech House. Pumping four-to-the-floor kick, driving 909 hi-hats, a syncopated acid bassline, and atmospheric dark synth stabs. Energetic and hypnotic, festival dance floor energy, 130 BPM
TrackType: Music, VocalType: Instrumental, Genre: Chillwave. Dreamy synth-pop with shimmering reverb-drenched guitars, pulsing synth arpeggios, and gentle trap-style hi-hats. Nostalgic, bittersweet, summer aesthetic, 100 BPM
🎸 Stems & Solo Instruments small-music

Start with TrackType: Instrument to maximize isolation. Great for creating backing tracks, B-roll music, or layering in your video editor.

TrackType: Instrument, a moody, melancholic solo piano with rich room reverb. Slow, reflective phrasing with occasional sustain pedal resonance. Classical and cinematic in tone.
TrackType: Instrument, Format: Duo, energetic Latin percussion duo with tight conga and bongo interplay, deep tumbao pattern, bright and lively with a live-recorded studio feel.
💥 Sound Effects Prompting small-sfx
✓ DO
Describe the source (what object), the action (how it's triggered, how long), and the acoustics (mic placement, room character, processing). Use TrackType: SFX prefix. Keep duration short (1–5s for one-shots).
✗ DON'T
Use long durations for short sounds — SFX are brief by nature. Don't mix music and SFX in the same model call; switch to small-sfx specifically for effects.

EXAMPLE PROMPTS — click to use:

TrackType: SFX. A satisfying UI button click — a tight, clean, bright tap with a short attack and very fast decay. Slightly warm and wooden, like a mechanical keyboard key, with no reverb.
TrackType: SFX. A whoosh sound effect — air rushing rapidly from left to right, starting subtle and building to a sharp peak. Clean, fast, and smooth, like a swipe transition in a mobile app.
TrackType: SFX. Deep cinematic bass drop impact — a massive sub-bass thud with a long rumbling tail, recorded with a close-mic setup in a large reverberant hall. Powerful and low-end heavy.
TrackType: SFX. Notification chime — three ascending soft piano notes, gentle and friendly. Short duration, bright tone, light reverb, no harsh transients. Think iPhone-style notification.
✂️ Inpainting & Continuation small-music · small-sfx

Upload an existing audio file and regenerate only a specific region. Great for fixing a section, swapping a sound, or extending a clip. For continuation: set Mask Start = end of your audio and Mask End = desired total length.

Fixing a bad section

Set mask to the problematic region (e.g., 15s → 20s). Use the same prompt style as the surrounding audio to maintain continuity.

Extending audio

Set mask_start = length of clip, mask_end = desired total. The model uses context from the existing audio to continue naturally.

Style transfer a section

Mask a large region and use a contrasting prompt. The transitions will blend with surrounding content.

Context matters most

The surrounding audio guides the output more than the prompt. Mask a tiny region and the result will closely match the original.

EXAMPLE PROMPTS for inpainting:

A punchy live-recorded drum fill leading into the chorus, energetic with tight snare and crashing cymbal
TrackType: SFX. A short transitional whoosh that bridges two scenes, matching the existing audio's energy level
🔄 Audio-to-Audio (Style Transfer)

Upload a seed audio and use a prompt to transform its style. The Noise Level slider controls how much the original is changed: 0.1 = very close variation, 0.9 = radical transformation.

Timbre transfer

Change the instrument but keep the melody. Try: input violin.wav → prompt "Heavy metal electric guitar" → noise level 0.4

Genre shift

Upload a track and shift the entire style. Try: input pop.wav → prompt "Cinematic orchestral version" → noise level 0.65
