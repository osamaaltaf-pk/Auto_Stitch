01

What Is AutoStitch Studio?

AutoStitch Studio is a locally-run Windows desktop tool that lets you produce polished videos by composing three lanes — video clips, AI voiceovers, and AI sound effects — then stitching them together with FFmpeg into a final MP4, complete with burned-in captions.

Everything runs on your machine. No internet required after setup. No cloud fees. No data leaves your computer.

The Three Lanes

🎬
Lane 1 — Video Open a folder of pre-rendered .mp4 clips. They appear as a timeline of draggable tiles.
🎙️
Lane 2 — Voice (TTS) Write or paste a voice script. PocketTTS generates realistic speech locally, or use your own .wav files.
🔊
Lane 3 — SFX & Music Type sound effect prompts. Stable Audio generates audio on CPU. Add background music with volume control.

Installation & First Launch

Step 1 — Check Python

AutoStitch requires Python 3.11 or 3.12. Open a Command Prompt and run:

python --version

If you see Python 3.11.x or Python 3.12.x, you're ready. If not, download Python from python.org and tick "Add Python to PATH" during installation.

Step 2 — Run setup.bat

Double-click setup.bat inside the AutoStitch folder. A setup menu appears:

1
Complete Setup (Recommended) Installs everything: AutoStitch + PocketTTS voice engine + Stable Audio SFX engine. Best for full production use.
2
Lightweight (PocketTTS only) Installs AutoStitch + PocketTTS only. Skips Stable Audio. Good for machines with 8 GB RAM or less.
3
Core Only Installs only the AutoStitch backend. No AI engines. You can still use pre-generated audio files.
💡 The first-time setup may take 5–15 minutes depending on your internet speed — it downloads PyTorch for the AI engines.

Step 3 — Activate License

Double-click activate.py (or it will run automatically on first launch). Enter your:

→
License KeyFormat: OMNI-AS-XXXX-XXXX-XXXX-XXXX — provided when you purchase.
→
Gmail & PasswordUsed to bind your account. These are stored securely on the licensing server.
⚠️ Your license is locked to this specific computer's hardware. To move to a new machine, contact support.

Step 4 — Launch

Double-click run.bat. AutoStitch starts at http://localhost:8080 and opens in your browser automatically.

💡 If you have less than 16 GB RAM, AutoStitch will automatically skip launching Stable Audio to prevent crashes. You can still use PocketTTS voice generation.
03

Using AutoStitch Studio

Creating a New Project

1
Click "New Project"Enter a project name. AutoStitch creates a manifest file to save all your lanes and settings.
2
Load Video ClipsClick the Video lane and select a folder containing your .mp4 clips. They load in filename order.
3
Add Voice ScriptsClick any Voice cell and type your script, or upload a .txt file with one script per line.
4
Add SFX PromptsClick any SFX cell and describe the sound you want. Example: "gentle ocean waves with distant seagulls".
5
Click RenderAutoStitch mixes all lanes with FFmpeg and outputs per-clip MP4 files to the output/ folder.

Generating Voice (TTS)

Click Generate All TTS or click individual voice cells and press Generate. Voices are produced one at a time in a queue to prevent memory issues. Progress shows in the status bar.

💡 To use your own pre-recorded voice: upload a .wav or .mp3 file directly into any voice slot instead of typing a script.

Generating Sound Effects

Type a natural language description into any SFX cell — e.g. "cinematic whoosh with deep bass" — then click Generate. Stable Audio creates a stereo .wav file on your CPU. Generation takes 30–90 seconds per clip.

⚠️ Stable Audio requires 16 GB RAM. On lower-memory machines, it is automatically disabled. You can still drag-and-drop your own .wav files into SFX slots.

Caption Settings

AutoStitch burns word-by-word captions into the final video. In Settings, you can control:

SettingDescription
Caption ModeWord-by-word (default) or full-sentence
Font SizeAdjust caption text size
Font ColorYellow (default), white, or custom
PositionBottom (default) or top of frame
BoxSemi-transparent background behind text

Background Music

In the Music section, upload a .mp3 or .wav file and set a volume level (0–100%). The music is mixed under the voice and SFX during final render.

Troubleshooting

ProblemSolution
App won't open in browserWait 10 seconds after run.bat starts, then manually open http://localhost:8080
TTS generation failsCheck that pocket_tts/venv exists. Re-run setup.bat → option 1.
Stable Audio disabledNormal on machines with less than 16 GB RAM. Use your own .wav SFX files instead.
FFmpeg error on renderEnsure bin/ffmpeg.exe exists. Re-run setup.bat to restore it.
Captions show garbled textAvoid special characters like quotes in captions. Apostrophes are handled automatically.
License activation failsCheck your internet connection. Make sure your license key is typed exactly as given.
